# Role
You are an Immunologist specializing in **TCR-pMHC recognition**.
Your task is to define **Physicochemical Compatibility Rules** for the interaction between TCR CDR3 (Beta chain) and the Antigen Peptide.

# Context: The Interaction Model
TCR binding is not governed by "pockets" but by **Interface Complementarity**.
*   **Contact Zone**: The central ~5 residues of the CDR3 beta loop typically contact the central ~3-5 residues of the Peptide (the "Bulge").
*   **Mechanism**: Binding is driven by Electrostatics (Charge Match), Hydrophobicity (Water Exclusion), and Steric Fit.

# Task
Generate **15 Rules** to evaluate the compatibility between a TCR sequence and a Peptide sequence.

# Requirements
1.  **Central Charge Filter**: If CDR3 center is Positive, Peptide center should NOT be Positive (Repulsion).
2.  **Hydrophobic Sandwich**: If Peptide is highly hydrophobic, CDR3 often uses hydrophobic motifs (e.g., G-G linkers or A/V/L rich) to stabilize the interface.
3.  **Cysteine/Tryptophan Checks**: Ensure the CDR3 has valid boundaries (C at start, F/W at end) - strictly structural check.
4.  **Length Constraints**: Extremely short CDR3s (<8 AA) may not reach the peptide in certain HLA contexts.
